### Multiple Choice Questions (MCQ)

1. Which type of map **shows** countries, states, and borders?  
(a) Physical  
(b) **Political**  
(c) Thematic  
(d) All of the above  
> **Answer:** (b)

2. Latitude lines are also known as __________.  (a) Meridians  
(b) **Parallels**  
(c) Equinoxes  
(d) Tropics  
> **Answer:** (b)

3. The **Equator** is located at __________ degrees latitude.  
(a) $23.5^\circ$  
(b) $45^\circ$  
(c) $90^\circ$  
(d) $0^\circ$  
> **Answer:** (d)

4. Moving eastward across a longitude of $15^\circ$ changes the local time by __________ hour(s).  
(a) 0  
(b) **1**  
(c) 2  
(d) 3  
> **Answer:** (b)

5. Which map would display **rainfall** patterns over a region?  
(a) Physical  
(b) Political  
(c) **Thematic**  
(d) All of the above  
> **Answer:** (c)

### Fill in the Blanks

1. Lines that run east‑west and measure north‑south distance are called __________.  
> **Answer:** **Latitude**

2. The imaginary line at $0^\circ$ longitude is called the __________.  
> **Answer:** **Prime Meridian**

3. Each time zone is based on a __________ of longitude.  
> **Answer:** **15°**

4. The intersection of the **Equator** and **Prime Meridian** is at coordinates __________.  
> **Answer:** **0°, 0°**

5. A map that shows **population** distribution is an example of a __________ map.  
> **Answer:** **Thematic**

### One Word Answer

1. What is the name of the line at $0^\circ$ latitude?  > **Answer:** **Equator**

2. What is the line at $0^\circ$ longitude called?  
> **Answer:** **Prime Meridian**

3. What are the horizontal lines used for north‑south positioning?  > **Answer:** **Latitude**

4. What are the vertical lines used for east‑west positioning?  
> **Answer:** **Longitude**

5. What type of map shows natural features like mountains?  
> **Answer:** **Physical map**
